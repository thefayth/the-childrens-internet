## What This Project Is

The Children's Internet is a protected public project surface and presentation microsite for a child-first internet concept. It imagines browsing, clubs, social permissions, family visibility, AI screening, and human moderation working together as one environment designed around childhood.

## Why It Matters

Most online spaces were not built for children first. This project asks what would happen if childhood safety, wonder, belonging, and accountable care were foundational instead of afterthoughts.

## How It Works

The current microsite presents the idea through four public paths:

- Vision and emotional framing.
- Interactive demo scenarios.
- Safety-system explanation.
- Pitch mode for a structured presentation.

## Project Highlights

- Child-safe storybook visual system.

- AI plus human moderation as a care model.
- Trust-gated social access.
- Clear public/private boundary.

## Current Status

The project is currently a static presentation microsite, not a live backend product. Product systems such as accounts, verification, parent dashboards, moderation queues, and reporting are modeled in demo content rather than implemented as live services.
